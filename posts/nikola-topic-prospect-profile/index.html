2024 NBA Draft Prospect Profile: Nikola Topic

Author

Dillon Siders

Published

June 10, 2024

In recent years we have seen an influx of foreign talent enter the NBA. Some of the best players in the league like Giannis Antetekounmpo, Joel Embiid, and Luka Doncic hail from outside the US. While not every country is producing tons of basketball talent some like France have produced tons of NBA players over the last few years.

Another country like France that has seen an increase in NBA success among their players is Serbia. Not only is Serbia the home of the current best player in the world Nikola Jokic, but also other NBA role players like Bogdan Bogdanovic and Nikola Jovic. This summer Nikola Topic looks to become the newest Serbian to make his mark in the NBA.

Nikola Topic has been a top prospect in the 2024 draft since the start of the cycle. The 6’6 point guard provides a lot of the things NBA front offices covet in a lead initiator especially since he won’t turn 19 until after the draft. Topic had an up-and-down year showcasing his on-ball potential at Mega before getting called back to Red Star where a couple of injuries derailed the end of his season. Topic’s combination of skills gives him the potential to return top-5 value to a team that drafts him provided they properly manage his development.

Scoring/Shooting Right now Nikola Topic’s scoring is predicated on his driving ability. This year playing with Mega, Topic lived in the paint. He is able to pressure the rim without traditional athleticism instead using his IQ, pace, and craft. He can use creative finishing angles and good body control to counter rim protectors. Topic also has great command of the pick and roll and is great at using it to get downhill. It should be noted that Topic is exclusively finishing below the rim and his underwhelming athleticism could cause struggles early on in the NBA.

Outside of Topic’s finishing, he does not have another area where he can consistently generate offense. His questionable jump shot will allow teams to sag off and go under screens early. Not having a reliable jump shot also limits the impact Topic has off-ball. There are some positive indicators that his jump shot will improve like all-around good touch and a high free throw percentage, but his shooting form has left some questions.

While I do not think that Topic has an ugly jumper by any means there are some improvements he could make. The biggest one for me is the arc on his shots. It was easy to tell the makes from the misses just by the arc alone. When Topic got air under the ball his jumper looked normal, but most of the time his shots were closer to a line drive than a pop fly. Even without a consistent 3-point shot, I can still see Topic becoming an impactful NBA player, but without some way to keep the defense honest there could be a major cap on his ceiling. Passing/Playmaking The area where evaluators are most impressed by Topic is his passing. While at Mega he starred as their primary ball handler and offensive initiator. His elite vision and quick decision-making perfectly complement his skills as a driver, finisher, and pick-and-roll ball handler. Topic best displays this when he’s able to use his gravity as a driver to draw in defenders before dishing to an open shooter or big.

Few in the class rival Topic’s vision, but nobody can match his passing versatility. In my opinion, Topic is the best passer/playmaker in this class because of the sheer amount of passes he can make and his current age. It is rare to see players have the amount of playmaking skill Topic has at his age. He finds teammates in transition, spotting up, rolling to the rim, and along the baseline/in the dunker spot.

The final thing that makes Topic a great playmaker is his natural basketball feel. When Topic has the ball in his hands it feels like has absolute command of the offense. It feels like he knows what a defense is going to do before they do it. His high understanding of how defenses react and live dribble passing skills let him make passes other players do not even dream of attempting.

Defense Even though I am a big believer in Nikola Topic’s offensive game his defense leaves me with a lot of questions. Right away Topic is a subpar athlete with a negative wingspan which immediately limits his defensive upside. His athletic limitations mostly hurt him as an on-ball defender. Poor lateral agility and technique make it unlikely Topic ever guards on ball and poor measurables make off-ball impact questionable at best.

Over the year Topic registered a 1.8 steal% and 0.5 block% which are underwhelming, but the sell on Topic has always been his offense anyway. I definitely think there are paths for Topic to survive on defense off the ball. His high basketball IQ and feel should eventually allow Topic to make an impact off the ball as long as he maintains proper positioning. Teams can also make his life easier on defense by surrounding him with plus defenders.

Overall Outlook There has been some unfortunate news around Topic as of late that might affect his stock on draft night. Recently it was revealed that Topic suffered a partially torn ACL at the international combine in Treviso Italy. On top of this Topic had another knee injury earlier this year. It has been stated that this should not cause his draft stock to drop, but the NBA is crazy, especially around draft time. As long as the team that selects Topic gives him proper treatment and rest he should be able to debut sometime this upcoming season. Even if he doesn’t play next year we have seen rookies sit out their first year and still become great NBA players.

At the start of this draft cycle Topic was as high as my No.1 player in the draft. Though I have cooled some on Topic I still have him as a top-10 player in this year’s class. I would be willing to bet on his passing/playmaking skills, natural basketball IQ, and feel for the game especially since he won’t turn 19 until after draft night.

In the NBA I can see two paths that Topic’s career can take. Obviously, there are other outcomes besides the ones I see, but those paths are less clear than the ones I’m mentioning. The first is his high-end outcome where he is a team’s lead guard and initiator. In this role, he would be a high-volume pick-and-roll ball handler who uses his IQ and vision to set up teammates. Topic’s low-end outcome probably looks similar to a Josh Giddey-type player. Even without a reliable jump shot I find it hard for a player as smart as Topic to not find some role in the NBA.
